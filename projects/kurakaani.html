How I used Atomic Design principles to redesign Nepal's Superapp

DESIGN SYSTEM, COMPONENT LIBRARY

Quick Summary

The Atomic Design principle uses the concept of atoms and molecules to set up a Design Language System. Having worked on maintaining and creating design systems, I created one for Nimbuzz Kurakaani, Nepal's Super App, as a part of their application redesign.

"I enjoyed doing small activities that were also fun, moreover I was aware of when I was wasting my time and when I was actually idle" — Shubham, 26 · Manager at an MNC

Project Description

Nimbuzz Kurakaani is Nepal's digital platform that houses multiple services like shopping, chat, quizzing, money transfer & much more in one app. To cater to the growing user base, the application's interface and experience needed an overhaul.

I was commissioned as a UI Designer to Redesign the app by creating a component-based system. The features in the application attract a relatively younger audience, usually young adults and people in their 20s. Therefore, all the decisions made during the redesign have been made keeping in mind the target audience.

Goals of the redesign

  • Update the design language to have a modern and cheerful vibe
  • Create a system that is scalable and easy to maintain
  • Inculcate various platform guidelines to improve overall designs


My Approach

Having worked on multiple projects where I've used a component library, I started with an Atomic Design approach to creating a component library.



Outcome of the project

  • With the creation of a design library, the time to design flows and screens reduced by ~60%
  • The hand-off process got easier as the components were defined in one place and developers could pick the specs from there.
  • The flows in the application were more refined and clear compared to the earlier version. This meant easy communication across teams.
